# Top 10 Economics Questions to Drive the Draft

1. Which productivity, income-distribution, and wellbeing statistics from Uruguay, Switzerland, Norway, Singapore, New Zealand, Botswana, and Qatar most clearly illustrate the performance frontier the book is aiming for?
2. How can Uruguay design a sovereign wealth strategy that blends Norway’s oil model, Singapore’s strategic investing, and Botswana’s resource governance without replicating their institutional pitfalls?
3. What tax architecture on income, consumption, and cross-border flows would most efficiently finance expanded public investment while mapping clear winners and losers among households, firms, and foreign investors?
4. Under what conditions does additional public debt raise long-run growth versus trigger destabilising risk premiums, and how do the focus countries’ experiences inform the thresholds?
5. What portfolio of technology policies—subsidies, regulation, public labs—best accelerates productivity while cushioning labour-market dislocation, and how should Uruguay balance the interests of workers, entrepreneurs, and incumbents?
6. Which combination of universal income, targeted transfers, and bureaucracy simplification maximises net welfare gains, and how do administrative cost curves compare across the reference countries?
7. What evidence-based education reforms could close Uruguay’s skills gap relative to Singapore, Switzerland, and New Zealand, and what economic returns should we expect over a 5-, 10-, and 20-year horizon?
8. Which climate and energy policies deliver the highest emissions reductions per dollar while preserving competitiveness, based on the empirical record of Norway, New Zealand, and Qatar?
9. What impact metrics should citizens track yearly to verify progress—growth per capita, median wealth, emissions, fiscal balance—and how will transparent dashboards keep reasonable coalitions stronger than unreasonable opposition?
10. Which data-backed counterarguments from sceptical personas (e.g., libertarians, degrowthers, fiscal hawks) could undermine the proposals, and how must the economic analysis pre-emptively address them?
