
# Ideological skeleton

1. **Reader persona (current prototype)** – Reform-minded analyst seeking a rational design playbook for competent governance. Future editions can repackage the same backbone for citizens, policymakers, or investors without changing substance.
2. **Through-line narrative** – Societies can and must do much better when governance is treated as an improvable system; civic competence is both possible and urgent.
3. **Empirical anchors** – Benchmark every major claim against statistics and institutional contrasts from Uruguay, Switzerland, Norway, Singapore, New Zealand, Botswana, and Qatar to show design, not culture, drives performance.
4. **Tension management** – Present politics as power struggles between reasonable and unreasonable ideas; stay lucidly realistic about incentives while keeping optimism alive.
5. **Reinvention criteria** – Define ambitious-but-implementable reforms around simplicity, institutional coherence, fiscal neutrality, measurable outcomes, and awareness of path dependence.
6. **Stakeholders** – Map winners and losers for each proposal to explain coalition dynamics and acknowledge the transparency gap parliaments often leave.
7. **Research cadence** – Treat evidence gathering as integral to argument formation; each chapter should flag datasets, case studies, and benchmarks to collect.
8. **Impact metrics** – Dedicate space to tracking progress beyond GDP, covering efficiency, opportunity access, trust, and competence density.
9. **Counter-arguments** – Stress test ideas with adversarial personas (populist, bureaucrat, libertarian, fatalist) so objections are surfaced internally first.
10. **Appendix as commentary** – Close with a reflective essay on democracy and privacy that reconnects systemic reform to personal autonomy and information power.
