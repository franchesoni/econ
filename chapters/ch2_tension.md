Technology helps humans produce more of the same given the same effort or produce new things that weren't possible before. The first is equivalent to producing the same with less work. That's why tech and jobs are in tension: some technological advancements disrupt jobs. I like the example of the alarm clock: in the industrial revolution, you payed someone to go wake you up at a given hour. It's interesting to question who woke up this worker. In any case, that job disappeared because the worker was replaced by a multitude of alarm clocks. But at the same time, new things were made possible and countless new jobs were created. There are two sides of this argument.
