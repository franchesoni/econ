
# Definitions

## Economy
We say economy when we refer to the production and distribution of resources.
The production and distribution is done somewhere or by some people, usually a country.

## Resources
Essentially goods, services, or investment assets (rough categories). 
In short, anything that we might want. 

## Capital
Resource-generating resources.

## Wealth
Resources in someone's property, specially capital.

## Transaction
Transfer of resources between two parties. Might be bilateral or not (like gifts).
Many times one party provides money (see next).

## Money
One type of resource (with or without intrinsic value) that
- is generally accepted in transactions (medium of exchange)
- can be stored / saved (storehold of value) 

## Economic equality
The produced resources are distributed or assigned to people or organizations of people.
Equality means that the difference in the resources assigned to different people is very small.
Inequality is that the difference in the resources assigned to different people is very big.
This is usually represented by plotting the percentage of the resources assigned to the x% that receive the least resources vs x.

## Tax
A claim on resources by a state. 
Usually when a transaction happen, the state claims some resources related to it.
But it can be tied to something else: land, wealth, transfers, companies, imports, etc.

## Tariff
A tax on imports (international reception of goods or services).

## (the) Rich (people)
People who have the most resources (the most wealthy).

## (the) Poor (people)
People who have the least resources (the least wealthy).

## Income 
The resources assigned to someone.

## Labor
When a person does some activity in exchange for resources.

## Worker
Someone whose income is related (mostly) to labor. 

## Capitalist 
Someone whose income is related (mostly) to their capital.
